Introduction

Agriculture is Kenya's Backbone

Agriculture contributes over 33% of Kenya's GDP and employs more than 70% of the rural population. Yet, smallholder farmers face unprecedented challenges due to climate change, with unpredictable weather patterns threatening crop yields and food security.

Traditional farming knowledge is no longer reliable as climate shifts accelerate, making modern weather forecasting essential. However, this information remains largely inaccessible to rural farming communities who lack smartphones, internet access, and technical literacy.

Mkulima Alert bridges the gap between meteorological science and rural farming communities.

The Challenge

Climate Change is Disrupting Farming

Traditional farming knowledge is failing as climate patterns shift beyond historical norms, leaving farmers vulnerable.

"I don't have Google. I don't read charts. I just need to know … will it rain tomorrow?"

— A Kenyan Smallholder Farmer

Unreliable Traditional Knowledge

Climate patterns are shifting faster than generational knowledge can adapt, making traditional farming cues increasingly unreliable.

No Smartphone Access

Most rural farmers use basic phones without internet, making app-based weather services inaccessible.

Technical Jargon

Weather forecasts are presented in technical formats that require interpretation skills most farmers don't have.

Language Barriers

Weather information is rarely available in local languages that rural farmers understand.

No Consistent Channel

Farmers lack a reliable, consistent channel for receiving weather information when they need it.

Limited Resources

Most farmers cannot afford expensive data plans or premium weather services.

Sample Messages

What Farmers Receive

Simple, actionable weather messages in Kiswahili with English translations

Kiswahili

Mkulima Alert: Kesho utapata mvua nguvu katika eneo lako. Panda mbwa za majani mwanzoni mwa Aprili.

English

Mkulima Alert: Tomorrow you will receive heavy rain in your area. Start planting vegetables early in April.

Kiswahili

Mkulima Alert: Jua kali sana leo! Hakikisha umenyunyiza mimea yako asubuhi na jioni.

English

Mkulima Alert: Very hot sun today! Make sure to water your plants in the morning and evening.

Kiswahili

Mkulima Alert: Msimu wa kupanda ni wa Aprili-Mei. Subiri mvua za kwanza kabla ya kupanda.

English

Mkulima Alert: The planting season is April-May. Wait for the first rains before planting.

Kiswahili

Mkulima Alert: Mvua kubwa inatarajiwa wikiendi hii. Chelewesha upanzi hadi Jumanne.

English

Mkulima Alert: Heavy rain expected this weekend. Delay planting until Tuesday.

Data Protection

Your Data, Your Rights

We are committed to protecting farmer data and privacy

Mkulima Alert is committed to responsible data handling practices that prioritize farmer privacy and security. All data collection and usage follows strict ethical guidelines and complies with Kenya's Data Protection Act (2019).

  • Voluntary opt-in with informed consent - farmers choose to join
  • Secure data storage with encryption - protecting farmer information
  • No sharing of personal data with third parties without explicit consent
  • Farmer data anonymized for AI training - protecting individual privacy
  • Right to deletion - farmers can request their data be removed at any time
